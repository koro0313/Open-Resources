# Open Resources

This is my take on making education as accessible as possible.

Currently, if anyone wants to study a subject, their best options are:
*   Relying on free sources found online, which are scattered all over the internet.
*   Buying or borrowing a book to study from it.
*   Relying on their school or college, if they are enrolled.

But we are technologically advanced enough to support everyone with free digital resources—if we can put our hunger for money and competition aside.

---

## A Free, Community-Driven Platform

What I propose is a completely free, community-driven platform for study materials. **In fact, you are reading this document on an early version of that very platform right now!**

*   **Simple Digital Files:** Anyone can upload resources—whether they are notes, books, or entire courses—made up of clean, digital text files (markdown).
*   **Integrating Video Lectures:** Creators can link lecture videos from YouTube directly into these files and structure them with additional notes.
*   **Win-Win for Creators:** People who view these lectures through the platform will still give views to the original creator on YouTube, supporting them too.
*   **Ready for the Future:** Because these resources are clean digital files, they have great potential in this modern era and can be fully utilized by artificial intelligence (AI).

---

## How It Works for Learners, Teachers, and Creators

Anyone looking to study a subject can find study materials here. The community's response over a resource determines how reliable and accurate it is.

*   **Multi-Language Support:** Since these are digital text files, we can easily support a large number of languages.
*   **No Age Barriers:** Anyone of any age can study about anything from here.
*   **Printable Books & Printing Services:** Users can download study materials as PDFs and print them out as physical books for themselves. Additionally, services can be built to provide printed versions of these materials as physical books for people who are willing to pay for printing.
*   **Fair Revenue Sharing:** If a commercial company makes money using these open resources (like selling printed versions), they should give a portion of their revenue to the original author from whom they earned.
*   **Collaborative Improvements:** If you see room for improvement in a study resource, you can copy (clone) it, make your changes, and request the original author to include those changes. Alternatively, you can publish your own new version for the public to read. This way, the community can work together to make resources more accurate or create different versions depending on their specific needs.

## For Technical People

Yes, I am proposing a **GitHub-like platform for study resources**. 

The platform itself will be fully open source, and all the notes and other data uploaded here will be openly available for anyone who wants to contribute or build something new out of it. 

The code is completely open-source and licensed under the **AGPL-3.0** license, meaning technical people who believe in this vision can start contributing to it right away.
