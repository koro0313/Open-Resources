# Open Evaluation

If anyone can study and prepare themselves on any subject using Open Resources, a natural question arises: **How can they gain hands-on, real-world experience, evaluate their progress, and prove to themselves and others that they are truly educated and hold real value?**

The answer is **Open Evaluations**.

---

## What is an Open Evaluation?

An Open Evaluation is a practical, in-person event, test, course, or competition where you perform to demonstrate your knowledge and skills in a subject. Based on your performance, you are issued a certificate specially curated for you.

Instead of a single, rigid grading system, evaluations can take many creative and practical forms:
*   **Practical Events:** Hackathons, competitions, workshops, debates, and group discussions.
*   **Courses & Lab Work:** In-person training and workshops where you work with physical equipment.
*   **Traditional Formats:** Standardized written tests, for areas where they are still the most effective method.
*   **The Lecture Circle:** A unique concept where you deliver a lecture on your area of expertise, and others do the same. As you explain concepts, answer doubts, and respond to others' presentations, your own understanding deepens, allowing evaluators to assess your command over the subject. These circles can feature students from the same domain or a highly diverse mix of fields.

---

## The Value of Certificates: A Self-Regulating Trust System

Under this model, the **certificate holds the value**, and that value is defined jointly by the certificate's owner and the reputation of the issuing agency.

### How Trust is Maintained:
*   **Earned Value:** A certificate that is only found among the most skilled people in an industry becomes highly valuable. Conversely, a certificate handed out to everyone—regardless of capability—loses all value. To get a better certificate, you simply improve your skills and challenge yourself with higher-level evaluations.
*   **Combatting Corruption:** If an evaluation agency becomes corrupt and begins selling certificates or passing unworthy candidates, the community will quickly notice when unqualified individuals hold their credentials. Consequently, employers and the public will stop trusting certificates from that agency starting from the date of the corruption, and the agency will naturally die out.
*   **Historical Reliability:** The system is smart enough to handle change. If a reputable agency goes bad, certificates issued *before* that transition remain highly valued, while certificates issued *after* the decline are treated as untrusted.
*   **Direct Endorsement:** Certificates don't just have to come from organizations. If you work on a project and impress a highly respected professional in the corporate or academic world, they can directly issue a certificate of endorsement to you.

## Practical Example: A Medical Student's Path

To see how this works in practice, imagine a medical student who has studied all their theory through Open Resources:
1.  **Proposal:** The student wants hands-on laboratory experience, so they send a proposal to a lab agency of their choice, outlining their practical requirements.
2.  **Scheduling & Fees:** The lab agency responds with available dates and a transparent list of charges.
3.  **Hands-on Learning:** The student pays the fee, attends the lab course, and performs the practical tasks.
4.  **Certification:** At the end of the course, the student is evaluated and receives a certificate reflecting their actual capability in the lab.

## Why Open Evaluations are Better

By decentralizing testing and credentials, this model solves the most frustrating aspects of the current examination system:

*   **No Examination Bottleneck (The Funnel):** Currently, all students are forced to take the same exams at the exact same time, creating massive crowds, extreme pressure, and administrative stress. In this model, you take evaluations whenever you feel ready. Testing is distributed throughout the year, making it far easier to manage and less stressful for students.
*   **No Monopoly:** Right now, pathways like JEE (engineering) or NEET (medical) have a total monopoly. If they become corrupt or inefficient, students have no other options. In this model, multiple evaluation agencies compete with one another, driving them to offer better opportunities, fairer fees, and higher-quality assessments.
*   **No Outdated Curriculum:** Since students want certificates that are valued by employers, they will naturally avoid agencies evaluating outdated syllabus. Outdated evaluations will adapt to modern needs or naturally die out.
*   **Price Transparency:** Agencies are encouraged to keep their charges transparent. If an agency hides its costs, the community will simply review it poorly and opt for transparent competitors.
*   **No Age Restraints:** Anyone of any age can learn from Open Resources and attempt an Open Evaluation, supporting lifelong learning.
*   **Diversity and Adaptability:** Every student can carve out their own custom path, leading to a much more diverse and resilient workforce.

## The Digital Portfolio Platform

All Open Evaluations and earned certificates are recorded on a single, community-driven, open-source digital platform. 

For the student, this works like a **self-developing portfolio**. As you focus on your growth and complete evaluations, the verified proof of your achievements is automatically added to your profile.

### Future Possibilities for Technical People:
Because the platform is open source and the certificate records are digital, developers can build powerful tools on top of this data:
*   **Skilled Worker Search Engines:** A highly reliable search tool for employers to find candidates with specific, verified practical skills.
*   **AI Portfolio Evaluation:** AI-based systems that verify a candidate’s certificates, evaluate the credibility of the issuing agencies, and provide a reliable estimate of the candidate's true capability.
