# New Schooling System

The current schooling system is built like a narrow funnel with two wide openings. It forces students of all interests down a single, rigid path that prioritizes memorization over true understanding.

![Schooling Funnel](/open-education-docs/schooling-funnel.png)

In this traditional model:
*   Students are forced to memorize ("mug up") history, geography, science, and language concepts to score marks, regardless of whether they understand them or not.
*   Mathematics is taught without any connection to its practical, real-world use.
*   Students are discouraged from questioning and exploring, and are instead rewarded for staying quiet and conforming.
*   Many exit the system unsure of what they want to achieve, having had their natural curiosity systematically suppressed.

To fix this, I propose a new schooling system split into two phases: **Primary Schooling** and **Secondary Schooling**.

---

## 1. Primary Schooling (Up to Class 5)

In the primary section, the absolute focus is on **curiosity, exploration, and character development**. Children are encouraged to discover what truly interests them rather than learning a pre-determined curriculum of subjects they may never need.

### Core Areas of Focus:
*   **Character & Social Development:** Teaching children how to present themselves, interact with others, react to different situations, and develop a strong civic sense.
*   **Expression & Language:** Focusing on how children convey their ideas and whether they can understand text they read on their own.
*   **Physical Health & Creativity:** Prioritizing sports, health, creative arts, critical thinking, and real-world problem-solving.
*   **Exploration:** Encouraging children to explore different topics of interest, ask questions, and watch meaningful, educational movies.
*   **A Healthy Balance of Memory:** Memorization is recognized as a tool for basic learning, but it is not treated as the primary goal of education.

### Evaluation:
There are no stressful exams. Instead, students are evaluated on their overall developmental milestones:
*   Their communication and conversation skills.
*   Their grasp of fundamental concepts.
*   Their participation in physical activities.
*   Their commitment and growth in their personal areas of interest.

Regular attendance in this period is highly encouraged to build these foundational soft skills.

---

## 2. Secondary Schooling (Class 6 to 10 or 12)

As students enter secondary school, the focus shifts toward **independence**. The goal is to make students capable of self-education. Regular daily attendance becomes less important; what matters is how much a student is actively developing themselves.

To replace the fixed yearly curriculum, this model proposes a **flexible stage-based learning system**.

### The Subject Stage Model
Subjects are divided into progressive stages (from 0 to 12) rather than grades. A student can learn subjects at their own pace and might be at different stages in different subjects:

*   **Mathematics:** Stage 0, 1, 2, 3 ... 12
*   **Social Studies (SST):** Diverges from basics (Stages 0–3) into specialized paths like **Geography** (4–12) and **History** (4–12).
*   **Science:** Diverges from basics (Stages 0–5) into **Physics**, **Chemistry**, and **Biology** (6–12).

Under this model, students build custom profiles based on their strengths and interests:
*   **Science-Focused Student:** Might be at *Math 5, Science 6, History 2, Literature 4*.
*   **Humanities-Focused Student:** Might be at *Math 2, History 6, Geography 7, Civics 4, Finance 2*.

### On-Demand Evaluations
Instead of standardized exam seasons, students study independently using Open Resources and attend open lectures at school. When they feel ready to move to the next stage in a subject, they opt for an evaluation in their school.

### A Meaningful Report Card
The yearly report card is a reflection of actual progress, listing:
1.  **Completed Subject Stages:** The current level reached in each subject.
2.  **Real-World Achievements (Highly Valued):** Actual accomplishments such as building a project, doing a case study, writing a poem, performing well in a competition, or taking an external open evaluation. 

By valuing real-world achievements over simple stage listings, students are discouraged from racing for marks and are instead motivated to study subjects to build, create, and solve real problems.

---

## The Role of the School

Under this model, the school is no longer a factory for exams. Its duties are reorganized to support the student:

*   **Resource Provision:** Providing physical infrastructure like labs, libraries, tools, and workspaces.
*   **Active Guidance:** Conducting interactive classes and doubt-clearing sessions to help students learn.
*   **Hosting Evaluations:** Organizing practical events, tests, and open evaluations.
*   **Life-Skills Curriculum:** The only mandatory curriculum from the school's side is teaching essential life skills—such as soft skills, how economics works, and how the legal system functions.
*   **Service Collaboration:** If a school lacks a certain resource or service, it is the school's duty to collaborate with external organizations or open evaluation agencies. In return, the partner organization is promoted as the official service provider.

---

## Moving to Higher Education (College)

By the time a student reaches the end of secondary school (10th or 12th standard), they will have a clear idea of what they want to achieve, backed by solid life skills. 

### The College Role:
*   **Refinement & Practice:** The role of college is to refine the student's chosen direction and provide them with hands-on, professional, real-world experience.
*   **No Long-Term Lock-in:** Unlike the current system where you are stuck in a college once you enroll, students will have the freedom to switch colleges after a cooldown period (e.g., 6 months). This allows students to gain diverse experiences across different institutions and find the environment that suits them best.
