# What is Open Education Model

This new education system is built on a **hybrid model**: learning materials, evaluation agencies, and school operations are community-driven and completely independent of government control, while the government provides legal recognition for certificates and funding/operation for the reservation system.

This model is built upon **4 main pillars** that restructure how learning materials are accessed, how students are evaluated, how schools are organized, and how support is allocated.

---

## This Model Has 4 Main Pillars

### 1. Open Resources
*   **An attempt to make education as accessible as possible:** This pillar aims to remove all barriers, making knowledge available to everyone regardless of location, background, or wealth.
*   **An open source community-driven platform:** A platform with completely free resources for anyone to educate themselves on any subject. Anyone can contribute, curate, and benefit from these learning materials—fully independent of government funding, control, or curriculum approval.

### 2. Open Evaluations
*   **A new take on testing:** Designed to replace typical, stressful exams with a more meaningful and reliable way to evaluate someone's genuine expertise through independent, competing evaluation agencies.
*   **Self-paced testing & legal recognition:** Students can prepare themselves at their own pace and opt for an evaluation when ready. Government provides official legal and professional recognition to these certificates for jobs, licensure, and college admissions, without controlling agency operations or curriculum.

### 3. A New Schooling System
*   **Freedom to explore:** A schooling system where students have the complete freedom to explore different subjects and study what interests them at their own pace, free from rigid mandatory syllabi.
*   **Inspire and support:** Schools will be expected to inspire students, support them, and provide them with whatever they need to grow, rather than focusing on rote memorization.
*   **Real-world preparation & character development:** On the other hand, the school will primarily focus on every student's character development, confidence, sports, and other essential life skills. It will teach them finance and prepare them to navigate the real world successfully.

### 4. A New Reservation System
*   **Needs-based physical verification:** A government-funded and operated system where dedicated physical verification agents visit students in person and issue targeted aid, irrespective of caste and based entirely on their actual condition and needs.
*   **Agent accountability:** Agents operate under official authority and funding, backed by a community reporting and auditing layer. If any misused reservation is found, we can trace it back to the agent who issued it and audit every other reservation they have issued. This makes it extremely risky and hard to abuse reservations, ensuring a fair ground for everyone.

---

## The Hybrid Funding & Recognition Split

At a high level, this model draws a strict line between learning and governance: the community builds and manages Open Resources, Open Evaluations, and Schooling independently to protect flexibility and rapid innovation, while the government funds the Reservation System and legally recognizes certificates to grant them real-world weight and sustainable national scale.

---

## The Problems This Model Solves

By reorganizing education around these four pillars, this model directly addresses and resolves several major issues:

*   **Outdated Syllabus:** The community-driven nature of the platform automatically filters out outdated syllabus in favor of up-to-date and relevant content.
*   **High Financial Barriers:** Provides top quality and completely free study resources for everyone, making quality learning free of charge.
*   **Unequal Access:** Ensures everyone gets a fair chance to receive an education.
*   **Age Restrictions:** Anyone of any age can educate themeselves, facilitating lifelong learning.
*   **Abuse of Reservations:** Fixes the abuse of the reservation system via physical verification, government funding, and strict agent accountability.
*   **Systemic Corruption:** Ensures no one unworthy or corrupted survives in the system.
*   **Forced and Rigid Learning:** Gives students the freedom to explore their interests and grow at their own speed.
*   **Lack of Practical Skills:** Prepares students for the real world by teaching financial literacy, confidence, and character development.
