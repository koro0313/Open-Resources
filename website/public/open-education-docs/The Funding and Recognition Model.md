# The Funding and Recognition Model

To understand how the Open Education Model operates at a national scale, it is crucial to understand the distinct boundary between what the **community builds and runs** versus what the **government funds and legally recognizes**.

This hybrid model combines the agility and freedom of community-led innovation with the legal authority and financial backing of public infrastructure.

---

## The Hybrid Split: Who Does What?

| Ecosystem Pillar | Operated & Built By | Funded & Recognized By | Role of Government |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Open Resources** | Volunteer Community & Educators | Independent Community Use | None (100% free of control or approval) |
| **Open Evaluations** | Independent Agencies & Organizations | Government (Legal Recognition) | Grants official validity for jobs, licensure & admissions |
| **New Schooling System** | Local Schools, Teachers & Communities | Independent / School-run | None (No mandatory government curriculum sign-off) |
| **New Reservation System** | Government Operations & Agents | Public Government Funds | Operates verification infrastructure, audits & penalties |

---

## 1. Community-Run: Learning, Teaching, and Evaluation

The core learning mechanisms—**Open Resources**, **Open Evaluations**, and the **New Schooling System**—are built, maintained, and operated entirely by the community of educators, developers, schools, and learners.

*   **No Curriculum Bottlenecks:** Government does not approve, review, or dictate what is taught on Open Resources or how schools structure their learning.
*   **Freedom to Innovate:** Open Evaluation agencies design their own practical assessments, events, and lab courses without waiting for bureaucratic permission.
*   **Speed of Adaptation:** Outdated syllabi can be updated immediately by the community rather than taking years to pass through government committees.

---

## 2. Government-Backed: Recognition and Funding

While the government is kept completely out of curriculum creation and educational gatekeeping, its participation is actively sought in two critical areas where legal authority and nationwide resources are indispensable:

### A. Legal & Professional Recognition of Certificates
*   **The Problem It Solves:** Without formal recognition, new evaluation agencies face a "cold-start" trust problem, and learners face a legal ceiling where only traditional credentials (e.g., NMC, UGC, AICTE) count for regulated professions or higher education.
*   **The Government's Role:** Government grants official legal and professional validity to certificates issued by vetted Open Evaluation agencies. A recognized certificate counts for government and private job applications, professional licensure (medical, legal, engineering), and college admissions.
*   **Crucial Distinction:** Recognition is granted based on transparent, objective, and auditable criteria—**not** control over how an agency teaches or tests.

### B. Funding and Operating the Reservation System
*   **The Problem It Solves:** A volunteer community effort cannot reliably conduct physical in-person audits, income verifications, and fraud prosecutions for millions of students at a national scale.
*   **The Government's Role:** The government funds and operationally runs the **New Reservation System**. Dedicated agents conduct in-person visits to issue needs-based reservations, supported by legal authority to penalize fraud.
*   **Community Oversight:** The community maintains an active auditing and reporting layer to ensure agents remain honest and accountable.

---

## Why This Boundary is Essential

If government controls content, the system recreates the rigid, monopolistic gatekeeping it was designed to replace. Conversely, if government is completely excluded, certificates lack legal standing for regulated fields and reservation verification lacks nationwide enforcement authority.

By maintaining **community independence for learning** and **government partnership for recognition and funding**, the Open Education Model achieves maximum freedom for learners with full real-world validity.
